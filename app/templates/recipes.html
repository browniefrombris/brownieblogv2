Egg Pasta


Eggs
Plain (all-purpose) flour
Semolina flour (optional)

Use 50g of flour per person and 1 egg per 100g of flour.
Use 3 parts plain flour and 1 part semolina if available.
1. Pour flour directly on to clean and dry benchtop and form a well.
2. Crack the egg(s) directly into the well and gently introduce the flour into the egg with a fork.
3. Steadily combine the flour and eggs together until a rough dough forms.
4. Continue to combine the dough until smooth, then kneed for 5-10mins until soft and silky.
5. Let rest for 30mins before rolling.

Pancakes


Self-raising flour (1.5 cups)
Sugar (1 tbsp)
Milk (1.25 cups)
Egg (1)
Butter (3 tbsp)
Vanilla essence (1 tsp)

Check pan temperature by flicking water on to it - water should sizzle and dance on surface and not immediately evaporate.

Top with:
- Bacon
- Maple syrup
- Lemon juice & sugar
1. Melt butter (3 tbsp) in microwave.
2. Add sugar (1 tbsp) and combine.
3. Add egg (1) and vanilla essence. Then whisk together.
4. Add milk (1.25 cups) and combine.
5. Add self-raising flour (1.5 cups). No need to measure precisely - just add until correct consistency.
6. Pour or spoon onto hot pan.
7. Flip pancakes when top surface has numerous bubbles.

Honey Miso Turkey Meatballs

Taste.com

Turkey mince (500g)
Spring onion (aka scallion aka green onion aka green shallot?) (1)*
Ginger (2 tsp + 1 tsp)
Garlic (2 cloves)
Miso paste (3 tsp + 4 tsp)
Breadcrumbs (1/4 cup)
Honey (2.5tbsp)
Mirin seasoning (1/4 cup)
White wine vinegar (1/4 cup)

- The original recipe for this dish used proper Mirin (rice wine) which is alcoholic and less sweet than Mirin Seasoning, but also not available at Australian grocery stores
- The original recipe also calls for rice wine vinegar, not white wine vinegar. I bought white wine vinegar in the belief that it was a close enough substitute, and maybe more versatile?
- *I used the white half of the spring onion for this recipe and the green half for a vietnamese salad to pair with it.
Meatballs
1. Thinly slice spring onion (1) and place in mixing bowl
2. Add breadcrumbs (1/4 cup) + ginger (2 of 4 tsps) + garlic (2 cloves) + miso paste (3 tsp)
3. Add turkey mince (500g)
4. Add salt and pepper if interested
5. Combine all ingredients together
6. Shape into meatballs and place on plate in fridge while sauce is prepared

Sauce
1. Whisk together honey (2.5 tbsp) + mirin seasoning (1/4 cup) + white wine vinegar (1/4 cup) + ginger (1 of 3 tsps) + miso paste (4 of 7 tsp) in a bowl

Cook
1. Cook meatballs in pan on medium-high heat until cooked through
2. Add sauce and cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid is like syrup
3. Serve and eat
