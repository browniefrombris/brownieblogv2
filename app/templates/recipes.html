Egg Pasta

Eggs
Plain (all-purpose) flour
Semolina flour (optional)

Use 50g of flour per person and 1 egg per 100g of flour.
Use 3 parts plain flour and 1 part semolina if available.
1. Pour flour directly on to clean and dry benchtop and form a well.
2. Crack the egg(s) directly into the well and gently introduce the flour into the egg with a fork.
3. Steadily combine the flour and eggs together until a rough dough forms.
4. Continue to combine the dough until smooth, then kneed for 5-10mins until soft and silky.
5. Let rest for 30mins before rolling.

Pancakes

Self-raising flour (1.5 cups)
Sugar (1 tbsp)
Milk (1.25 cups)
Egg (1)
Butter (3 tbsp)
Vanilla essence (1 tsp)

Check pan temperature by flicking water on to it - water should sizzle and dance on surface and not immediately evaporate.

Top with:
- Bacon
- Maple syrup
- Lemon juice & sugar
1. Melt butter (3 tbsp) in microwave.
2. Add sugar (1 tbsp) and combine.
3. Add egg (1) and vanilla essence. Then whisk together.
4. Add milk (1.25 cups) and combine.
5. Add self-raising flour (1.5 cups). No need to measure precisely - just add until correct consistency.
6. Pour or spoon onto hot pan.
7. Flip pancakes when top surface has numerous bubbles.

Honey Miso Turkey Meatballs

Taste.com

Turkey mince (500g)
Spring onion (aka scallion aka green onion aka green shallot?) (1)*
Ginger (2 tsp + 1 tsp)
Garlic (2 cloves)
Miso paste (3 tsp + 4 tsp)
Breadcrumbs (1/4 cup)
Honey (2.5tbsp)
Mirin seasoning (1/4 cup)
White wine vinegar (1/4 cup)

- The original recipe for this dish used proper Mirin (rice wine) which is alcoholic and less sweet than Mirin Seasoning, but also not available at Australian grocery stores
- The original recipe also calls for rice wine vinegar, not white wine vinegar. I bought white wine vinegar in the belief that it was a close enough substitute, and maybe more versatile?
- *I used the white half of the spring onion for this recipe and the green half for a vietnamese salad to pair with it.
Meatballs
1. Thinly slice spring onion (1) and place in mixing bowl
2. Add breadcrumbs (1/4 cup) + ginger (2 of 4 tsps) + garlic (2 cloves) + miso paste (3 of 7 tsp)
3. Add turkey mince (500g)
4. Add salt and pepper if interested
5. Combine all ingredients together
6. Shape into meatballs and place on plate in fridge while sauce is prepared

Sauce
1. Whisk together honey (2.5 tbsp) + mirin seasoning (1/4 cup) + white wine vinegar (1/4 cup) + ginger (1 of 3 tsps) + miso paste (4 of 7 tsp) in a bowl

Cook
1. Cook meatballs in pan on medium-high heat until cooked through
2. Add sauce and cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid is like syrup
3. Serve and eat

Hot Cross Buns

Recipe Tin Eats | Video

Plain flour (4.25 cups + 1/2 cup)
Instant yeast (9g / just under 3 tsp)
Sugar (1/2 cup)
Allspice (2 tsp)
Ground cinnamon (2 tsp)
Salt (1/2 tsp)
Butter (50g, melted)
Milk (1.5 cups)
Egg (1, beaten)
Sultanas (1.5 cups)
Orange zest (1 orange worth)
Water (5 tbsp + 2 tsp)
Jam (1 tbsp) - traditionally apricot

If you like you can buy cling film made from potatoes, not plastic, here.
I use an old bread bag with the corner cut instead of a piping bag.
Consider warming milk to lukewarm prior to adding. Original recipe calls for this but I'm not convinced of its value.
Bun dough and 1st rise
1. Combine flour (5.25 cups) + yeast (9 g) + sugar (1/2 cup) + allspice (2 tsp) + cinnamon (2 tsp) + salt (1/2 tsp).
2. Add butter (50g, melted) + milk (1.5 cups) + egg (1, beaten) + sultanas (1.5 cups) + orange zest (1 orange worth).
3. Knead for 10 mins until smooth and silky.
4. Place in large oiled bowl, cover with tea towel, and leave in a warm and dry place for 1.5 hours (or until double in size).

Forming buns
1. Remove towel and punch dough to deflate.
2. Place on floured bench and roughly shape into a log 30-40cm long depending on how large you want the buns to be.
3. Cure into 12-16+ equal pieces, again depending on how large you want the buns to be.
4. Lay each slice flat on bench then gather 4 corners together to form a parcel. Roll parcel between palms to form ball. Watch video link for demonstration.
5. Place dough balls on oiled oven tray, evenly spread.

2nd rise can be done either of these two ways:
1. Cover tray with tea towel and let buns rise again for 45mins (or until 1.75x size).
or
1. Cover tray with cling film and leave in fridge overnight.
2. Remove from fridge 2.5 hours prior to baking to allow to return to room temperature and rise to 1.75x size.

Crosses
1. Mix flour (1/2 cup) + water (5 tbsp).
2. Spoon into piping bag and pipe onto buns, taking care to ensure the mixture crosses from one side of the bun all the way to the other. Pipe mixture along a whole row in one continuous movement.

Baking
1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
2. Bake buns for until golden brown (~20mins).

Glaze
1. Combine jam (1 tbsp) + water (2 tsp).
2. Microwave for 30 sec, then mix.
3. Remove buns from oven and transfer to cooling rack.
4. Brush with glaze while still warm.
5. Cool, then eat.

Bread (White loaf)

Plain flour (500g)
Instant yeast (6g)
Bread improver (6g)
Sugar (6g)
Salt (6g)
Water (320ml)
Canola oil (20g)

This recipe is the one on the back of the Wallaby baker's flour bag. I use this flour but I imagine the recipe works well enough with any plain flour.
I use this bread improver but using it at all is probably unnecessary.
Leave to cool and dry out a little on the bench before storing. I find if I wrap it in a bag and store it too soon it grows mould very readily, and I'm assuming this is to do with the moisture.

1. Combine flour (500g) + yeast (6g) + bread improver (6g) + salt (6g) + sugar (6g) in a bowl.
2. Add canola oil (20g) + water (320ml), and combine together.
3. Empty dough out on to bench and knead for 5-10mins.
4. Return to clean and oiled bowl, cover with towel, and leave in warm place until it doubles in size.
5. Once doubled in size, punch down and knead on bench for another 2 mins.
6. Shape into an oblong and place into oiled bread tin.
7. Cover with towel and leave to double in size again.
8. Preheat over to 220°C once dough is nearly doubled in size.
9. Bake for 20-25mins until dark brown on top.
10. Transfer to cooling rack.
